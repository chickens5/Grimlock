ML Data Strategy — Tiered Roadmap
You're currently in the metadata-only phase (105 cultivars, lineage known, but no cannabinoid/terpene quantification). To build genuine ML models for CBD + minor cannabinoids + terpene optimization, you need:

Tier 1: Essential (Collect First) — Must-Haves
A. Cannabinoid Lab Assays — HIGHEST PRIORITY

Current state: Hash Holes has only THC + top-3 terpenes, no CBD, no minor cannabinoids
What to collect: Full panel per cultivar (THCA, THC, CBDA, CBD, CBG, CBC, CBDV, minor compounds)
Sample size target: 30–50 cultivars × 3 replicates each = 90–150 assays
Cost: ~$50–150 per test = $7,500–22,500 over 3–6 months
Why: This is your training target. ML optimizes what you measure; without CBD/minor cannabinoid data, any "CBD optimization" model is guessing.
B. Growth Environment Logs — HIGH PRIORITY

What to track: Temperature, humidity, VPD, CO₂, light PPFD, photoperiod, nutrients (pH/EC), growth stage milestones
Collection: Automated sensors (hourly) or manual (3x daily minimum during flower)
Sample size: 20–30 grow cycles across your cultivar set
Cost: $500–2,000 (sensors, if not already logging)
Why: Phenotype = genotype × environment. Can't build trait heritability models without environmental context.
C. Morphology & Phenotype Checkpoints — HIGH PRIORITY

What: Plant height, internode length, leaf color/shape, cola density, trichome % (weekly during flower)
Collection: Photos (backlit, macro close-ups) + measurements; 4–8 timepoints per cycle
Why: Leaf structure, plant morphology correlate with terpene and cannabinoid output; low-cost predictors.
Tier 2: High-Value (Parallel) — Accelerators
D. Lineage Validation & Parent Cannabinoid Profiles

Cross your 105 strains against public databases (Allbud, Leafly, CCBC)
Confirm parent UIDs and link to known cannabinoid profiles
Cost: Free (manual work) + lab tests if you want to assay known parents
Why: Enables genetic-distance ML features + lineage-informed predictions
E. Quality & Contaminant Profiles (optional, lower priority)

Heavy metals, pesticide residues, microbial, residual solvents
Usually included in premium lab panels (+$50–100 per test)
Why: Optimize for potency + purity (matters for concentrate yield); not foundational
Tier 3: Advanced (Defer for Now) — DO NOT START YET
F. Genomic Sequencing (SNP/VCF Data) — CONDITIONAL; DEFER

Whole-genome resequence or SNP panel on top 20–30 cultivars
Cost: $300–1,500 per cultivar; 6–12 months
Current blocker: You need 50+ cannabinoid phenotypes first; reference genome assembly alone won't enable predictive models
Timeline: Pursue after Phase 2 (100+ cannabinoid assays) if data shows strong genotype patterns
G. RNA-seq (Gene Expression) — DEFER; ADVANCED

Measure terpene synthase / cannabinoid synthase activity in flower tissue
Cost: $200–500 per sample
Too expensive now; only worthwhile after you've already profiled cannabinoids/terpenes phenotypically
Tier 4: External Data (Leverage Now)
Public datasources to augment your own data:

UC Davis CCBC: strain profiles + cannabinoid data (limited but curated)
Weedmaps/Leafly Community Labs: crowd-sourced lab results (messy, large volume, free)
Published papers: Search PubMed/Scholar for cannabis cultivar cannabinoid/terpene studies
NCBI GenBank / PhytoZome: Cannabis sativa v2.1 annotation (gene models linked to biosynthesis)

Why This Matters for Your Models
Without Phase 1 data: Models are phenotype-free. You're scoring cultivars on lineage + terpene tags alone (current state = good for early filtering, not genuine ML).
With Phase 2 data (100+ cannabinoid assays): Decision trees can learn patterns like "OG-lineage strains with <1.8 internode length tend to have CBD >2%." Real predictive power.
With Phase 3 data (validated replicates + genomics): Trait inheritance modeling; can predict F1 offspring cannabinoid profile from parents.
Bottom line: Your current implementation provides a solid foundation for phenotype+lineage scoring. Real ML breakthrough happens once you have 50+ cultivars with full cannabinoid panels and linked growth environment data.
